Traffic Signs & Signals

Understanding regulatory, preventive signs, and pavement markings

🔴 Regulatory Signs (R-series)

What are Regulatory Signs?

Red and white signs that show limitations and prohibitions. These tell you what you CANNOT do or MUST do. Examples: R-6 (No left turn), R-40 (Low beams required), R-48 (Loading zone).

⚠️ Preventive Signs (P-series)

What are Preventive Signs?

Yellow diamond signs that warn about dangers, hazards, or special conditions ahead. These tell you to BE CAREFUL. Examples: P-36 (Slippery surface), P-41 (Tunnel ahead), P-48 (Pedestrians), P-49 (School zone).

🛣️ Pavement Markings

What are Pavement Markings?

Lines and symbols painted on the road that regulate, warn, and guide traffic flow. Yellow lines (center), white lines (lanes), arrows (direction), and special markings. They work together with vertical signs!

Traffic Lights & Signals

Understanding traffic light rules and priority

▶️

Traffic Lights Video Lesson

Learn about red, amber, and green lights, arrow signals, flashing lights, and police officer override rules

⏱️ 3-4 minutes
💡 Green Light Checklist

Before proceeding on green, check: Arrows, Blocked intersection, Pedestrians, Police signals. Green doesn't mean GO - it means CHECK THEN GO!

⚠️ Most Important Rule

Traffic lights control the flow of traffic, but safety always comes first. Even with a green light, you must check that it's safe to proceed. Police officer signals override all traffic lights.

Basic Traffic Light Colors

Light Signal Meaning What to Do
🔴 Solid Red
Luz Roja 		STOP You must stop completely before the intersection. Do not proceed until the light turns green.
🟡 Solid Amber (Yellow)
Luz Ámbar / Amarilla 		CAUTION - Prepare to stop Stop before the intersection if your speed and location permit. If you're too close or stopping would be unsafe, proceed and clear the intersection quickly.
🟢 Solid Green
Luz Verde 		PROCEED Exception: If the intersection is blocked by other vehicles, do NOT enter, even if the light is green. Wait behind the line until there is space to clear the crossing.

Arrow Signals & Flashing Lights

Light Signal Meaning What to Do
🟢⬅️ Green Arrow
 Proceed ONLY in arrow direction You can continue with caution only in the direction of the arrow and from the lane that this arrow controls.
🔴⬅️ Red Arrow + Green Light
 Go straight, but NO turning You may go straight or in other permitted directions, but turning in the direction of the red arrow is prohibited.
🔴⚡ Flashing Red
 Equivalent to STOP sign Legally equivalent to a STOP (PARE) sign. Stop completely, check all directions, then proceed when safe.
👮 Police Officer Signal
 Officer OVERRIDES traffic light If a Police Officer signals you to advance, you must obey the officer, even if the traffic light is Red. The officer's hand signals have absolute priority over traffic lights.

Speed Limits

Maximum and minimum speed limits in Peru

💡 Quick Rule

Minimum speed = Half of maximum speed. Urban streets: 40 km/h max, School zones: 30 km/h max, Highways: 100 km/h max.

⚠️ GOLDEN RULE: Posted Signs Always Win

If you see a speed limit sign on the road, you MUST obey that sign - even if it's different from the default limits below.

Right of Way

Priority rules at intersections and special situations

▶️

Right of Way Video Lesson

Master priority rules at unregulated intersections, STOP signs, roundabouts, and pedestrian crossings

⏱️ 3-4 minutes
💡 Priority Hierarchy

1. Police signals (override everything) → 2. Traffic lights → 3. Traffic signs → 4. Right of way rules. Pedestrians ALWAYS have priority!

⚠️ Golden Rule

Right of way determines who goes first. Even if you have right of way, always check that it's safe before proceeding.

Unregulated Intersections

Situation Who Has Priority? What to Do
Two vehicles arrive simultaneously
 Vehicle from the RIGHT If a vehicle approaches from your right, you must yield. Think: "Right = Right of way"
Pedestrian at crossing
 Pedestrian ALWAYS Stop and yield to pedestrians crossing. Even if there's no traffic light, pedestrians have absolute priority.
Entering roundabout
 Vehicles already inside Yield to all vehicles circulating inside the roundabout. Wait for a safe gap, then enter.
Railway crossing
 TRAIN - Absolute priority Trains ALWAYS have right of way. Never try to race a train!
💡 Priority Hierarchy

1. Police signals (override everything) → 2. Traffic lights → 3. Traffic signs → 4. Right of way rules

Pedestrians ALWAYS have priority!

Driving Maneuvers

Overtaking, turning, lane changes, and parking rules

▶️

Maneuvers Video Lesson

Learn proper techniques for overtaking, lane changes, turning, and parking safely

⏱️ 3-4 minutes
💡 Golden Rules

Overtake on the LEFT. Signal FIRST, then change lanes. Urban turns: signal 20m before. Highway turns: signal 30m before. Signal the ENTIRE maneuver!

⚠️ Golden Rule for All Maneuvers

Every maneuver requires three steps: Signal first, Check surroundings, Execute with caution.

Overtaking / Passing

Situation Rule Important Notes
Which side to overtake?
 LEFT side only Always overtake on the LEFT side, then return to your original lane.
Discontinuous yellow line
 Overtaking permitted You can cross to overtake if it is safe to do so.
Continuous yellow line
 NO overtaking NOT permitted to cross for overtaking when the center line is continuous yellow.
Using horn to warn of overtaking
 PROHIBITED It is NOT permitted to use your horn to warn the driver ahead that you will overtake them.

Signaling Requirements

Road Type Signaling Distance Notes
Urban roads (city)
 At least 20 meters before Signal with directional lights 20m before turning
Highways (between cities)
 At least 30 meters before Signal with directional lights 30m before turning
Duration
 ENTIRE maneuver Signal throughout the turn, not just at the start
💡 Memory Tip

"LEFT to pass, Dashed = Yes, Solid = No. City = 20m, Highway = 30m. Signal the whole way through!"

Mandatory Documents

Essential documents you must carry while driving

💡 Key Point

You must carry 3 mandatory documents at all times: Identity Document, Valid SOAT, and Vehicle Identification Card. Virtual SOAT is accepted!

⚠️ Before You Drive

Make sure ALL required documents are in your vehicle. Police can request them at any time. Missing or expired documents = fines and penalties.

Document Required? Validity Important Notes
1. Identity Document
DNI o Pasaporte 		✅ REQUIRED Check Expiration Must be original, match license name
2. Driver's License
Licencia de Conducir 		✅ REQUIRED 5-10 Years Cannot drive even 1 day expired. Penalty: Fine + Cannot obtain license for 3 years
3. SOAT Insurance
Seguro Obligatorio 		✅ REQUIRED ⏰ 1 YEAR Digital version accepted. Covers medical costs for injuries. Cannot drive without valid SOAT.
4. Vehicle ID Card
Tarjeta de Identificación 		✅ REQUIRED ♾️ Permanent Must carry original card. Proves legal ownership.
6. Technical Inspection
Revisión Técnica (RTV) 		⚠️ CONDITIONAL ⏰ 1 YEAR Required annually for vehicles 4+ years old. Not required 0-3 years.

Sanctions & Penalties

IF... THEN... rules for violations and consequences

⚠️ Most Severe Penalties

Alcohol/drugs + accident/refused test: Fine + Cancellation + PERMANENT ban. Driving without license: Fine + 3-year ban. 100 points in 24 months: License suspension.

⚠️ Important

Traffic laws exist to protect everyone. This guide uses simple IF... THEN... format for easy memorization.

Driver's License Violations

IF you do this... THEN the penalty is...
IF you drive without a driver's license THEN: Fine + Cannot obtain license for 3 years
IF your license is expired/invalid THEN: You CANNOT drive at all (same as no license)

Point System

IF you do this... THEN the consequence is...
IF you commit certain violations THEN: Points are ADDED to your license
IF you accumulate 100 points within 24 months THEN: License SUSPENSION

Alcohol & Drugs

IF you do this... THEN the penalty is...
IF you are stopped with alcohol above legal limit OR under effects of drugs THEN: Fine + License suspension for 3 years
IF you have alcohol/drugs AND participated in accident OR refused test THEN: Fine + Cancellation + PERMANENT disqualification
⚠️ Never Drink and Drive

Alcohol/drug penalties are the MOST severe. You can lose your license permanently. Always choose a sober driver or taxi.

Common Violations

Violation Status
Don't stop completely at STOP sign Violation ✅
Use horn unnecessarily Violation ✅
Drive with engine in neutral Violation ✅
License plates in wrong place Violation ✅
💡 Recidivism Rule

If you commit the same violation within 12 months, you'll pay DOUBLE the fine.

Safety & Responsibility

SOAT, seat belts, lights, vulnerable users, and defensive driving

▶️

Safety & Responsibility Video Lesson

Essential safety rules including insurance, seat belts, proper lighting, and protecting vulnerable road users

⏱️ 3-4 minutes
🛡️ Safety Essentials

SOAT is mandatory (1 year validity). ALL passengers must wear seat belts. Urban: LOW beams. Highway: HIGH beams → LOW before crossing. Vulnerable users always have priority!

⚠️ Most Important Principle

Safety is EVERYONE'S responsibility. These rules exist to protect you, your passengers, and all road users. Following them isn't just about passing the exam - it's about saving lives.

SOAT & Seat Belts - Mandatory Safety

Topic Rule / Requirement
SOAT Insurance
Seguro Obligatorio 		Required to drive - Covers occupants AND third parties - Validity: 1 year - Cannot drive without it
Seat Belts
Cinturones de seguridad 		MANDATORY for ALL: Front seats (always) + Rear seats (if factory-installed) - Not just for driver!

Vehicle Lights - Proper Use

Location / Situation Light Type Required
Urban roads at night
 LOW beams (NOT 24/7 - only at night!)
Highways at night
 HIGH beams → Change to LOW beams BEFORE crossing opposite vehicles
Fog / Tunnel
 LOW beams (+ fog lights in fog)

Vulnerable Users & Emergency Vehicles

Who / Situation Rule
Vulnerable users
Usuarios vulnerables 		Pedestrians, children, elderly, disabled, cyclists - Always have priority, even if you have green light
Schoolchildren crossing / School bus
 STOP completely - Do not overtake school bus picking up/dropping off students
Emergency vehicles
 Yield ONLY when BOTH siren AND lights are on - No lights/siren = NO obligation

Driver Fitness & Safe Behaviors

Condition / Topic Rule
Sleepy from medications
 Refrain from driving
Distractions causing accidents
 Worries + Cell phone + Radio manipulation = All cause accidents
Technical inspection
 Every year - Starts from 4th year after manufacture
If accident occurs
 Notify insurance + Police station record - Do NOT abandon scene
🎯 Final Reminder

Safety is not optional - it's mandatory. SOAT protects everyone, seat belts save lives, proper lights prevent crashes, and respecting vulnerable users shows responsibility. These aren't just exam rules - they're lifesaving habits!
